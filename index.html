5 — WHAT THE CUSTOMER GETS

Client Experience: AI Harness vs SaaS/BaaS

Two different offerings for client companies. The AI Harness gives the company a private, tuned AI tool. SaaS gives a ready-to-use CRM/operations platform (based on Zan and Kikuichimonji). Both are built from our internal engineering work.

🤖 AI Harness Service

A tuned, private version of AI tools like ChatGPT or Claude — built on the client's own data, running on their own infrastructure, with governance controls. Everyone in the company uses the same product, each for their own needs.

What the client gets:
  • Everyone uses the same AI product — each department for their own needs
  • Better than public AI tools: grounded in company data, privacy-controlled, governance built-in
  • Uses whatever data the company has — no special data preparation required
  • Acts like a tuned, easier-to-use version of ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini
  • Runs on company-controlled hardware — data never leaves the organization
  • Training included: workshops, manager briefings, governance playbooks

📦 SaaS / BaaS Product

Two ready-to-use platforms based on our internal apps — Zan (CRM/Sales Management) and Kikuichimonji (Operations/Admin). Configured per client, multi-tenant.

Why These 3 Hires, Why These Dates

Hire #1 — Sep 2026: Sep–Dec has 5 active projects needing ~3 developers. With 3 existing developers plus this hire ramping up, we cover it. This hire takes on reservation/knowledge platform work while senior developers focus on the core AI infrastructure.

Hire #2 — Apr 2027: Jan–Jun 2027 has 5 active projects needing ~4 developers (including 3 large ones: customer-facing AI, shared handlers, backend separation). Second hire absorbs governance/staff-builder support so existing developers stay focused on the separation program.

Hire #3 — Jan 2028: Jul–Dec 2028 adds SaaS foundation and Corporate Bohr SaaS on top of ongoing separation work — 6 projects needing ~5 developers. Third hire takes on SaaS foundation while the team continues backend separation.

Schedule adjustment: 4 project starts (PLAT-01, APP-SEP-01, GOV-01, IAGPT-04) moved from December 2026 to January 2027 to match hiring pace. Impact is minimal — 1-month slip on multi-year projects, decision gate moves Feb→Apr 2027.

Why 1-at-a-time: The manager does most of the training. Each new hire needs about 2 months to get up to speed. Staggering prevents training overload and matches the natural ramp-up of the project schedule.

✅ What to look for

  • Solid development fundamentals — can read code, debug, write tests
  • Git/PR workflow familiarity (our governance depends on this culture)
  • Communication skills — cross-department coordination is frequent
  • Willingness to learn our stack and conventions
  • Full-stack capable — backend (Django/Go) + some frontend (Vue)
  • Ideal: 2-4 years experience, independent after 2-month training

❌ What's NOT required

  • AI/ML expertise — we're building that in-house
  • Kubernetes/microservices experience — we define our own architecture
  • SaaS platform experience — the SaaS foundation is greenfield
  • Senior architect-level — the manager sets architecture
  • Education industry knowledge — teachable on the job
